Cognite

Cognitive Impairment Diagnosis App

Roles: UI/UX Designer, App Developer, ML Researcher

As a research assistant at the Human Intelligence and Language Technologies (HiLT) Labs housed in the University of North Texas (UNT), we discovered a Machine Learning algorithm to diagnose various cognitive disorders in just seconds. Building upon the research findings, I designed and branded an app (Cognite) to bring cognitive diagnosis to the fingertips of millions suffering from cognitive impairment.

Programs Used: Microsoft PowerPoint, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Final Cut Pro

Code Language: Python (Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing)



Problem

Cognition Impairment is the leading disease that impacts US citizens right after heart disease and cancer. For adults over the age of 25, statistics show that 1 in 4 people have some level of cognition impairment, though many may not even be aware of their own possible impairments. The market for people seeking cognition help is astronomically greater than what it is portrayed to be by general medical media.

In our current medical realm, Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) can only be diagnosed with the use of an MRI scan paired with a radiologist analysis -- this process can take up to two weeks to and can cost over $4,000 for just one scan and reading.


Solution

Cognite is an app targeted to those who suffer from any variation of a cognitive disorder. The app gives patients their risk factor of having a cognitive disorder after a two-minute speaking test.

Our research (below) outlines how Natural Language Processing (NLP) can be used to reach a diagnosis in a fraction of the time it takes current methodologies of MCI diagnosis.
